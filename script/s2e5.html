INTERIOR BUNKER LOCKER ROOM
Wellington and Butler enter the locker room. Wellington fidgets as he looks around the room.

WELLINGTON
Look at this place. Can you believe someone lives here?

BUTLER
I don’t know. I am fond of the metal décor.

Wellington punches.

WELLINGTON
Decor? What I tell you about reading my Ladies Home Journal before I finish doing the word search?

BUTLER
Actually sir, it was your Homes and Gardens.

Wellington punches him again.

WELLINGTON
Even worse.
(beat)
Look at that mess over there. With all this robot filth around I’m at risk to catch some kind of avian tetanus syphilis.

BUTLER
Sir I don’t think that’s possible.

WELLINGTON
Don’t talk to me what with all your
immunities.

Sniffing sounds are heard.

WELLINGTON
What was that?

BUTLER
I didn’t hear anything.

Sniffing sound again.

WELLINGTON
Shhh. There it was again.
(beat)
Maybe it’s those dreaded Hardy Boys.

Nancy jumps out from behind the lockers.

NANCY
Not the Hardy Boys.

Wellington screams. Butler screams. Nancy screams. Wellington again with Butler. Nancy again with Butler. Butler continues to scream pauses and continues screaming.

Wellington punches Butler and he stops screaming.

Charles runs in.

CHARLES
What’s the matter?

Charles sees Wellington.

CHARLES (CONT’D)
Oh no, did I miss my 2:30 brick throwing appointment?

WELLINGTON
As usual Charles, I don’t have time for your crap. It has come to my attention that you have allowed Chavez to operate out of your… ahem… home.

NANCY
Let’s say it was the result of a mixture of long weekends in Tijuana and an elementary school book fair. How was I supposed to know kids have smaller body cavities that normal people and almost no understanding of free trade agreements.

CHARLES
Honey, you don’t need to explain anything to this man.

WELLINGTON
Well somebody, I don’t care who, better tell me there’s a drug farm upstairs!

CHARLES
If someone had decided to give us the money we’re owed from the Father’s will, we wouldn’t have to rent out the upstairs.

WELLINGTON
I can’t believe you would have the audacity to rent to that Chavez scum. Do you have any idea what drugs are doing to my town.

Charles looks at Nancy.

WELLINGTON (CONT’D)
Let me tell you the story about a young man who started off taking money out of his mom’s pocket book to get an extra candy bar. Eighteen years later he’s turning tricks in order to lick the backs of rats that lived old meth barrels.
(beat)
And do you know who that young man was?

CHARLES
Who?

WELLINGTON
You.

CHARLES
No it wasn’t.

WELLINGTON
It wasn’t?
(to Butler)
Who am I thinking of?

BUTLER
Frank Zappato.

CHARLES
I think I played cards with him once?

BUTLER
No you didn’t.

CHARLES
Don’t tell me who I don’t play cards with!

BUTLER
I made him up.

CHARLES
So you’re a traitor and a liar!

WELLINGTON
Enough. The point is this is my town and I want them out of here now!

CHARLES
Well this is MY home and I can rent to anyone I want.

Wellington screams in anger and punches Butler twice.

CHARLES (CONT’D)
Now if you don’t mind I need to gather up my bricks for our meeting later.

Wellington screams again, punches Butler, and runs out. Butler follows.

CHARLES
You know, Nancy. That was the first time I’ve ever been able to get under that man’s skin. And to think, all it took was your little…mix up.

NANCY
And drugs. Lots of drugs.

CHARLES
Yes dear. And drugs. Lots of drugs.
