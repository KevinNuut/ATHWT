EXTERIOR STREETS OUTSIDE OF ROBOT BUNKER

Mr. Wellington and Butler walk together down the road to the bunker's entrance.

MR. WELLINGTON
I don't think it is humanly possible to hate you more than I do right now.

BUTLER
Yes, sir.

MR. WELLINGTON
Did I, or did I not tell you that I wanted to ride those bumper cars?
The two of them stop in front of the entrance to the bunker.

BUTLER
Yes, you did sir. But-

MR. WELLINGTON
But nothing, son. You've got some nerve even speaking to me after disobeying a direct order from your boss.

BUTLER
Sir, that carnival was shutdown 5 years ago.

MR. WELLINGTON
I provide for you, don't I?

BUTLER
Yes, sir.

MR. WELLINGTON
You bet your sweet ass I do. And where do you think that money I don't pay you comes from.

BUTLER
Sir?

MR. WELLINGTON
Work, boy. Work. Every red cent I make, I earn.

BUTLER
Yes, sir.

MR. WELLINGTON
Do you know how hard it is for me to go all the way down to the mailbox everyday, take the check addressed to me out of it, have you drive me down to the bank or liquor store, if I get up late, and have them cash it?

BUTLER
I would imagine it is very hard, sir.

MR. WELLINGTON
Shut your sasshole.

Nina exits the bunker directly into Wellington and Butler's conversation. They both turn to look at her.

MR. WELLINGTON (CONT'D)
I thought I made myself quite clear, Nina. The Chavez family is not welcome in this town.

NINA
Truthfully Wellington, Mrs. Chavez doesn't care what a brain dead fool who can't grow a proper mustache thinks.

MR. WELLINGTON
I won't stand here and let you say an unkind word about my butler without me introducing him to you.

BUTLER
Hello. My name is-

Mr. Wellington turns and punches Butler.

MR. WELLINGTON
Shut up. No one is talking to you.

NINA
Frankly Wellington, you don't have a clue about what is going on in this town.

Mr. Wellington becomes irate and punches Butler twice.

MR. WELLINGTON
Yes, I do! I know the candy store owner gives out lollipops to babies or men pretending to be babies. I know the town puts formaldehyde in the drinking water for population control. And I know of a little motel off the interstate where they won't ask any questions when a young blond girl and a suave older man checked in for 15 minutes of growling at the badger.

BUTLER
Oooo juicy!

NINA
Don't start.

MR. WELLINGTON
Making some baskets.

NINA
Stop it.

MR. WELLINGTON
Crashing the custard truck.

NINA
What?

BUTLER
I believe he's referring to sex.

Nina and Wellington turn and punch Butler. Nina turns back to Wellington.

NINA
I thought I told you to never bring that up.

MR. WELLINGTON
How am just supposed to forget our time together?

NINA
You're making it sound like I was the one who chose to endâ€¦whatever it was we had.

MR. WELLINGTON
You can't be saying that I put end to us?

NINA
Of course I am. After THAT incident, you ran off to Thailand and came back with that little trophy.

MR. WELLINGTON
Watch what you say Nina.

NINA
At least I can talk.

Wellington stares at Nina. Nina stares back.

BUTLER
What is she referring to sir?

Wellington punches Butler. Wellington turns back to Nina, no longer in a pleasant mood.

WELLINGTON
(growls to Nina)
Get out of my town.

Nina turns takes two steps and turns back around.

NINA
Of course some people would say that this is no longer YOUR town.

WELLINGTON
What do you mean?

NINA
Well if it really was your town, how could the Chavez family own half of the bunker directly under your feet.

WELLINGTON
What are you talking about!?

Nina LAUGHS and walks off.

NINA
(over her shoulder)
Ask your sister-in-law, Nancy.

Wellington and Butler are left standing alone.

BUTLER
What is she referring to sir?

Wellington punches Butler.

WELLINGTON
I need to find out what's going on here.

Wellington heads into the bunker with Butler behind him.
