INTERIOR ABANDONED COLD WAR BUNKER

Sam and Charles stand in the main room of their dingy abandoned cold war bunker home.

CHARLES
And he's the main reason I can't wear snow pants anymore.

Sam's heard this several times before.

SAM
I know, Dad.

CHARLES
You can't trust a Wellington or they'll screw you. Screw you right to the wall. Now-

The largest robot of the family, CINDY, runs into the main room, crying. She is bulky, white, and Daddy's little girl.

Sam and Charles turn to look at her.

Cindy keeps sobbing until she sees Sam and Charles staring at her.

CINDY
Don't look at me!

Charles slowly walks over to her.

CHARLES
What's the matter, honey?

Cindy sobs between words.

CINDY
Connie told everyone at school that I'm the fattest girl in town.

Cindy breaks into another crying fit.

CHARLES
Awww...honey. Come on now. You're not fat.
(beat)
And technically, you don't have the reproductive organs to classify you as a girl.

Cindy sniffles a little.

CINDY
You're just saying that because you're my Dad.

CHARLES
Come on sweetie, you know the Father designed you in his own image.

SAM
You mean he wheezes when he climbs the stairs too.

CHARLES
Don't start, Sam. Cindy, you're perfect in my eyes.

SAM
Perfectly ass-heavy.

Cindy starts crying again.

CHARLES
Sam!
(to Cindy)
He didn't mean it, honey.

SAM
Yes, I did.

Charles turns to Sam and pulls out a bat.

CHARLES
One more time and you taste the pine.

He puts the bat away and turns back to Cindy.

CHARLES (CONT'D)
The truth is the Father made all of us with things we don't like about ourselves.
(beat)
I mean just look at these arms. They're pitiful and T-Rex like.

They all look over at a T-Rex standing by a wall. The T-Rex drops his head in shame and walks out of the room. They all turn back to their converstation.

CINDY
But why?

CHARLES
Well, if you liked everything about yourself, then what would you obsess over day in and day out?

SAM
Whether or not you can use spray cheese as toothpaste.

Cindy starts crying again.

CHARLES
Don't listen to him. He's just upset because he was an accident.

SAM
Huh?

Cindy snickers and sniffles.

CINDY
I never really thought about it that way.

CHARLES
Sure.
(beat)
You know when your mother was about your age she had a similar thing happen to her.

CINDY
Really? Mom?

CHARLES
Yep. Why don't you go ask her about it?

CINDY
Okay. Thanks, Dad.

Cindy walks out of the room.

CINDY (O.C.)
Mom!

SAM
What do you mean I was a mistake?

Charles turns to Sam.

CHARLES
Had to keep flapping those gums, didn't you? Now you get to see the finish on this bad boy up close and personal.

Charles pulls out the baseball bat again.

Cindy walks through the halls of the bunker.

CINDY
Mom!

Cindy turns a corner to see her mother, NANCY, standing in the corner.

CINDY (CONT'D)
Hey, Mom. Dad said that when you were my age, other girls would pick on you. How did you deal with it?

Nancy begins sniffing like a cocaine fiend.

NANCY
Drugs. Lots of drugs.
(beat)
Drugs are the only way to make all the voices stop.
(beat)
You want some?

CINDY
God, yes!
